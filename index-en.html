Project Description

CarbonLoop aims to create a modular, ultra‑low‑energy urban cooling and microclimate stabilization system that reactivates natural thermal processes within the city environment.

The technology reduces cooling energy demand, mitigates urban heat island effects, and introduces a new net‑zero category in clean‑tech urban infrastructure.

Technology

CarbonLoop does not rely on mechanical cooling, compressors, or energy‑intensive equipment. Instead, it reactivates the natural heat exchange between water, soil, and air, enabling up to 90–98% reduction in cooling energy demand.

The modular architecture allows rapid deployment in urban, industrial, and extreme climate environments.
